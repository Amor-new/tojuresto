Egusi Soup with Pounded Yam

A rich and savory West African stew made from ground melon seeds, spinach, and a variety of meats or fish.

Served alongside smooth pounded yam, this comforting dish is a staple in Nigerian cuisine.

Grilled Suya Skewers with Pepper Sauce

Juicy, thinly sliced beef or chicken grilled over an open flame and coated with a signature suya spice mix made from peanuts, paprika, and ginger.

This popular Nigerian street food is served with spicy pepper sauce and fresh onions, offering a smoky, flavorful kick that’s hard to resist.

Pepper Soup with Catfish

A fiery, aromatic broth made with a blend of traditional Nigerian spices and fresh catfish. This hearty soup is perfect for cold nights or when you're craving something spicy and comforting.

It’s a dish that warms you from the inside out and is a favorite in many Nigerian homes.

Ofada Rice with Ayamase

Ofada rice, a locally grown Nigerian variety, paired with Ayamase, a rich, spicy green pepper stew cooked with assorted meats, boiled eggs, and palm oil.

This dish is beloved for its bold, savory flavors, making it a true Nigerian delicacy.

Jollof Rice with Grilled Chicken

A West African classic, Nigerian Jollof rice is simmered in a rich tomato, pepper, and spice blend, giving it its vibrant orange color and irresistible aroma.

Served with perfectly grilled, succulent chicken, this dish is a crowd favorite, known for bringing out the best of Nigerian hospitality.
